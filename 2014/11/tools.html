Tools

2014, Nov. 16

All users have certain tools that they love and certain ones that they would rather bury. I'm going to go over the list of tools I use and why. This includes text editor, document reader, music player, irc client, and much more.

Text Editor: vim

Vim has been my editor from day 1 and I can always count on it. That being said, I have tried other text editors so that I don't get caught up in just one. A few reasons why I love vim: it's simple, it's fast, and it's easily customized.

Music Player: ncmpcpp

For my main music player, I prefer ncmpcpp. I've only recently switched to it. Before, I was using moc. I prefer ncmpcpp over moc because of the better features and the ncurses ui.

Document Viewer: mupdf

I chose mupdf as my document viewer because it is lightweight and I do not need any features other than simply viewing the pdf file. Mupdf is as it says, 'mu' (micro). It weighs in at just under 9MB in my /usr/bin.

Terminal Emulator: rxvt-unicode

Out of the other terms I've tried, I prefer urxvt most. xterm, terminator, rxvt, just didn't cut it for me. I love the unicode features and the 256colors. And, as always, the ability to customize it is a big plus.

Shell: zsh

I can't say I truly 'prefer' zsh over others as the only other shell I've used is bash. However, zsh has all that I really need and I haven't had a reason to try anything else.

IRC Client: weechat

As with other items on the list, I haven't tried many other IRC clients. I did use irssi for a while, but I couldn't say that I enjoyed it any more than I do weechat. My simple reason for using weechat is that it's easier to customize than irssi is.

Summary:

In closing, most of the tools I use are simply because I've either used them since I first touched linux or because they can be easily customized. Perhaps, in time, I will have a better reasoning.
