dwb

2014, Nov. 17

I first heard of dwb through z3bra on #nixers. I saw it through his screenshots and decided that it was time to try it out. Switching over from firefox was easy enough as dwb behaves similar to firefox with vimperator installed. One thing I do miss is RES. I'm not even sure if dwb has support for plugins or not.

What is dwb?

"dwb is a lightweight webkit browser that is highly customisable and can easily be configured through a web interface. It is mostly keyboard driven and is inspired by firefox's vimperator plugin" -- A paraphrase of the 'about' section from the site.

Why use dwb?

I first started using dwb (among other things) to see how well I could get around without using a physical mouse. After a few days of use, I realized that I enjoy it more than firefox, even sans plugins. Why? Aside from being based off of vimperator, dwb is much more lightweight than firefox and loads very quickly. As well, I feel it is easier to customise than vimperator.

Why should I use dwb?

You shouldn't. Not if you don't want to. If you're a vim* user, you'll probably enjoy it. I do. I also have some complaints about it, such as the lack of plugins or the inability to play a .webm but they're fairly minor complaints. If I want to open a .webm, I can simply save it and open it up in mplayer.

In Summary...

I would definitely recommend you to at least try dwb. If you don't like it, you can simply remove it and be done. If you do like it, then at least I did some good today.
